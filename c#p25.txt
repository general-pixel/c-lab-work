what is clamp
A clamp is a tool designed for the temporary and secure fastening of objects, applying inward pressure to hold them tightly together and prevent movement or separation. It finds common usage in tasks such as positioning components during construction or woodworking projects.

Rules for clamping:

Always use a piece of scrap wood between the clamp jaws and wooden parts to prevent damaging the part.
Apply clamp load perpendicular to the part to prevent slippage.
Always use at least two clamps to prevent rotation.
Do not overtighten the clamp. Overtightening can damage the clamp

What Are Guide Rails or Stops?
Guide Rails: Mechanical tracks or linear guides that allow the welding head or workpiece to move along a defined path with high precision.
Stops: Fixed or adjustable positioning devices that limit movement, ensuring the workpiece or torch is precisely located for each weld.
Importance in Automatic TIG Welding
Accuracy & Repeatability: Ensures each weld is performed in the exact same position, critical for high-quality welds.
Efficiency: Reduces setup time since operators can quickly position parts or the torch without manual measurement.
Automation Compatibility: Facilitates automated movement and consistent operation, necessary for robotic or CNC-controlled welding systems.
Types of Guide Rails & Stops
Linear Guide Rails:

Usually made of hardened steel or aluminum.
Can be paired with linear bearings or slides for smooth movement.
Available in various lengths and load capacities.
Adjustable Stops:

Mechanical stops that can be repositioned easily.
Often equipped with micrometers or digital readouts for precise setting.
Used to set specific positions for repeatable welding paths.
Magnetic or Clamping Stops:

Quick to attach and adjust.
Suitable for rapid repositioning of workpieces.
Digital or Programmable Stops:

Integrated with CNC or robotic systems.
Allow for automated, programmable positioning.
